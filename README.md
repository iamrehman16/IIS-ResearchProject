# Detection and Mitigation of Wormhole Attacks in IoUT

## Overview
This repository contains the research work for the Internet and Information Security (IIS) semester project focused on studying the **Internet of Underwater Things (IoUT)** and the security threats affecting underwater communication networks. The project specifically investigates the **Wormhole Attack**, one of the most critical routing attacks in underwater sensor networks.

IoUT systems are widely used for applications such as ocean monitoring, environmental sensing, offshore infrastructure management, and underwater surveillance. These networks rely on acoustic communication, which introduces challenges such as high propagation delays, limited bandwidth, and dynamic network topology. These constraints make IoUT environments particularly vulnerable to network-layer attacks.

---

## Research Problem
A wormhole attack occurs when malicious nodes create a communication tunnel between distant locations in a network and relay packets through it. This causes routing protocols to falsely assume that distant nodes are direct neighbors.

Such attacks can lead to:

- Packet interception
- Selective packet dropping
- Network partitioning
- Routing disruption
- Energy inefficiency in underwater sensor nodes

Due to the characteristics of underwater acoustic communication, detecting wormhole attacks in IoUT networks remains a significant challenge.

---

## Project Objectives
The main objectives of this research are:

1. Study the architecture and communication characteristics of IoUT networks.
2. Analyze the mechanisms and impact of wormhole attacks in underwater environments.
3. Review existing research on wormhole attack detection and mitigation techniques.
4. Perform a comparative analysis of multiple existing approaches.
5. Identify limitations in current solutions.
6. Propose an improved detection framework suitable for IoUT networks.

---

## Research Methodology
The project will follow the following research approach:

1. **Background Study**
   - IoUT architecture and communication models
   - Security challenges in underwater acoustic networks

2. **Attack Analysis**
   - Working mechanism of wormhole attacks
   - Impact on routing protocols and network performance

3. **Literature Review**
   - Study approximately 10 existing research papers
   - Focus on wormhole detection and mitigation techniques in underwater networks

4. **Comparative Analysis**
   - Evaluate approaches based on detection accuracy, overhead, and feasibility in IoUT environments

5. **Proposed Framework**
   - Suggest an improved or hybrid detection mechanism based on identified research gaps
